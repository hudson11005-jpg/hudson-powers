By generation

Where you are shapes what you should do

The best financial moves at 25 are completely different from the best moves at 55. Here's what actually matters at each stage of life.

01
Max your Roth IRA first
You're in the lowest tax bracket you'll ever be in. Pay taxes now at 22%, let it grow for 40 years completely tax free. $7,000/year compounding at 8% = over $1.8M by retirement.
Tax Strategy
02
Never leave free money on the table
If your employer matches 401k contributions — contribute at least enough to get the full match. It's an instant 50–100% return before a single investment is made. Nothing beats it.
Retirement
03
Time is your only unfair advantage
A 22-year-old investing $500/month will likely retire wealthier than a 32-year-old investing $1,000/month — contributing less total. Compound interest is an exponent. Start now.
Investing
01
Asset allocation is everything
In your 30s you can still take risk — 70–80% equities is reasonable. But start building your fixed income allocation. A 70/25/5 split of stocks, bonds, and cash is a strong foundation.
Portfolio
02
Don't confuse net worth with wealth
Your house, car, and business aren't investable assets. When planning for retirement, focus on liquid investable assets — brokerage accounts, IRAs, 401ks. Know the difference.
Planning
03
Tax loss harvesting starts now
Selling losing investments to offset gains isn't giving up — it's smart. Harvest losses throughout the year, reinvest in similar positions, and reduce your tax bill legally every December.
Tax Strategy
01
Start planning your Roth conversion ladder
In your peak earning years, you can't convert yet — brackets are too high. But plan for it. Every dollar in your Traditional IRA will face forced withdrawals at 73. Roth conversions in retirement fix this permanently.
Retirement
02
Rebalance — the market did it for you
After a bull run your 60/40 portfolio might be 75/25 without you doing anything. That means more risk than you agreed to. Review quarterly, rebalance when allocation drifts more than 5%.
Portfolio
03
Municipal bonds are your friend
At a high income bracket, a muni bond paying 3.8% beats a corporate bond paying 5.5% — after taxes. Always calculate tax equivalent yield before choosing fixed income. TEY = Muni Rate ÷ (1 - Tax Rate).
Fixed Income
01
Delay Social Security — it pays 8% per year
Every year you delay claiming past full retirement age (67), your benefit grows 8% — guaranteed. Waiting from 67 to 70 increases your monthly benefit by 24%, for life. If you're healthy, wait.
Social Security
02
RMDs will cost you — unless you plan now
At 73 the IRS forces withdrawals from your Traditional IRA — taxed as ordinary income, added to your AGI, potentially triggering IRMAA Medicare surcharges. Roth conversions before 73 are the only real fix.
Retirement
03
Don't sell — borrow, then pass it on
Selling appreciated assets triggers capital gains. Instead, borrow against your portfolio via a pledged asset line. At death, heirs inherit at today's market value — all historical gains disappear. Buy. Borrow. Die.
